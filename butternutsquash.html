Butternut Squash and Cauliflower Soup

  • Whole Butternut Squash
  • Garlic Cloves
  • 1/2 Shallot
  • 2 qts Stock (vegtable or Homeade Chicken)
  • Whole Head of Cauliflower
  • Collard Greens
  • 1 cup Rice
  • 1/4 cup Milk
  • 1/4 cup Greek Yogurt
  • TBSP Butter
  • Handfull of Dill
  • Olive Oil
  • Ground Turmeric
  • Salt and Black Pepper

Preheat Oven to 400F

Mince garlic and shallots. Peel and medium dice squash, reserve the seeds for later.

Add a tablespoon of olive oil, garlic, and shallots to a soup pot. Stir occasionally until the garlic starts to lightly brown. Add squash and stir, allow to cook for 2 minutes. Add stock and a handful of dill and bring to a simmer.

While the soup begins to come to a simmer, break down the cauliflower head into very small florets (about Â¼ inch big), remove the stems and add to the pot of soup. The stems have tons of flavor and nutrients. Take the florets and toss in a tablespoon of olive oil, salt, pepper, and ground turmeric. Spread out on a foil lined baking sheet and bake for about 20 minutes until brown on the edges, toss occasionally. Make sure to line the pan with foil or you may dye your baking sheet yellow from the turmeric.

Take the squash seeds, clean and dry them. Toss with olive oil, salt and pepper. Bake on a foil lined baking sheet for about 7 minutes until crisp.

Allow soup to simmer until cauliflower stems and squash are fork tender. Turn off heat and allow to cool for 5 minutes, remove the dill. During this time, remove the stem of the collards and chop into bite sized pieces. Once soup has cooled, add to a blender with about 5 sprigs of fresh dill and blend until smooth.

Return blended soup to a low heat and continue stirring. Add rice to the soup and allow to cook all the way through, about 15 minutes. Once the rice is cooked, add milk and yogurt. Stir until fully combined. At this point, add the butter and collards, stir until butter is fully melted and combined. Add the cauliflower to the pot and stir in. let flavors meld together for about 5 minutes.

To serve, top with fresh dill, roasted squash seeds, and a spoonful of yogurt. Enjoy!

The soup with thicken, if reheating it is best to add a small amount of stock to make it less thick.
