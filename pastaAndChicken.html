Fresh Pasta with Rosemary Chicken

For the Pasta

  • All Pupose Flour (Quanto Basta or "until it feels right")
  • Eggs
  • A pinch of Salt

For the Chicken

  • Chicken Thighs (bone in)
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Rosemary
  • Olive Oil

For the Sauce

  • Canned Whole Tomatoes
  • Oaky Red Wine
  • Garlic

Make a volcano out of the flour and create a well in the center. Crack your eggs into the well and beat, incorporating little bits of flour at a time. Roll out your dough and knead for 10 minutes. Let rest, wrapped in plastic for at least 30 minutes to hydrate the dough. Then start rolling and shaping into whatever shape suits you!

Heat up olive oil in a pan. Put rosemary in a blender with salt until homogeneous. Rub salt mixture on chicken and crack fresh pepper. Put chicken skin side down in the oil until skin is crispy. Then flip and cook through, remove from the pan. In this pan make the sauce.

Smash garlic and add to leftover oil/chicken fat. Let get lightly brown. Add in whole tomatoes and smash gently with wooden spoon. Cook until thickened. Add red wine to loosen sauce and deglaze the pan. During this time boil your fresh pasta for about 2-3 minutes. Add pasta to the sauce directly with tongs so some pasta water is transferred as well. Serve with chicken on top and enjoy!
